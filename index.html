Michael Phelps

Michael Phelps is a retired American swimmer and the most decorated Olympian of all time, with a total of 28 medals to his name, including 23 gold medals. Phelps was born on June 30, 1985, in Baltimore, Maryland, and was introduced to swimming at a young age by his sisters, who were also competitive swimmers.
Phelps made his Olympic debut at the age of 15 in the 2000 Sydney Olympics, where he became the youngest American male swimmer to compete in the Olympics in 68 years. However, it was at the 2004 Athens Olympics where Phelps first became a household name, winning six gold medals and setting several world records.
Phelps continued to dominate the sport of swimming, breaking numerous world records and winning multiple gold medals at the 2008, 2012, and 2016 Olympics. He retired from competitive swimming after the 2016 Rio Olympics, leaving behind a legacy as one of the greatest athletes of all time.
Aside from his incredible achievements in the pool, Phelps has also been open about his struggles with mental health and has become an advocate for mental health awareness. He has also been involved in various philanthropic endeavors, including the Michael Phelps Foundation, which promotes healthy and active lifestyles for children.

Favourtie Sports

Swimming

Swimming is a popular recreational activity and a competitive sport that involves moving through water by using various techniques, such as freestyle, breaststroke, backstroke, and butterfly. It is a low-impact exercise that provides a full-body workout and helps improve cardiovascular health, muscle strength, and endurance. Swimming can also be a relaxing and therapeutic activity, providing a sense of weightlessness and reducing stress on the body. Whether in a pool, lake, or ocean, swimming is a versatile and enjoyable activity for people of all ages and skill levels.

Favourite Video Game

Words With Friends

Words With Friends is a popular mobile game developed by Zynga, a social game developer known for creating several other hit games like Farmville and Zynga Poker. Words With Friends is a multiplayer word game that allows players to challenge their friends or play against random opponents to create words on a game board. The game is similar to Scrabble, where players must use letter tiles to create words and score points based on the letters' values and placement on the board. The game has several features like in-game chat, the ability to see previous moves, and different game modes, including solo play, practice mode, and tournaments. Words With Friends has been downloaded millions of times and has become a popular social game for people of all ages.

EDUCATION

Towson High School

Michael Phelps attended Towson High School in his hometown of Baltimore, Maryland. He did not attend college and instead chose to pursue his swimming career full-time after the 2004 Olympics. However, Phelps has remained involved in education initiatives and has spoken publicly about the importance of education and learning.

He has also started his own foundation, the Michael Phelps Foundation, which focuses on promoting healthy and active lifestyles for children, as well as water safety and swimming education. Additionally, Phelps has taken courses at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, where he trained with renowned swimming coach Bob Bowman.
